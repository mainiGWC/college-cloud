FAQ's

You don't have to take any community college classes outside of your high school, but we highly recommend you doing so! Taking community college classes can boost your GPA as well as help you take classes which you find interesting that aren't offered at your high school. There are no downsides to taking community college classes outside of your high school!

Any that you're interested in! It's important to show colleges that you're well-rounded and have multiple interests. Activities are also a good way to figure out what kinds of careers you're interested in. Being involved in academic clubs can also help colleges see that you're invested in learning which can never hurt!

For 4-year universities, the average GPA is 3.75. For community college, 2.0 GPA's and above are accepted.

For community college, it is still important to do your best in school and take all of the classes that interest you, but you don't need to take as rigorous AP's or stack up multiple additional community college courses. In preparation for a 4-year university, it is best to take as many AP's and additional community college courses as you can, without overworking yourself. 4-year universities are generally more rigorous than community colleges, so keep that in mind when choosing which classes to add to your schedule.

Getting involved in a sport shows that you have collaboration skills and are a well-rounded individual. It is not necessary for you to get involved in a sport if you are disinterested or if you don't have the time. Many college preparation guides recommend being involved in a sport, but if you have equivalent experience from a different activity (being part of a group for a volunteering experience, being in a club with a specific purpose which includes working together, etc.) then you don't need to worry!

Make sure to stay organized, don't overwork yourself, and have fun! It's important to stay organized in high school with either a planner or an equivalent method. Time management is a primary aspect of high school life, you need to make sure you have enough time to finish and do all of your assigments before the due dates. If you're taking AP courses or additional community college classes, organization is a MUST. It's also important to remember that you're a human who needs to rest. Don't overload yourself with so many activities and classes that you no longer have time to do other things! High school is also an amazing time to hang out with your friends and do different things with them. Make sure that you don't have all work and no play! Enjoy your high school experience, and don't stress, as long as you try your best, you'll be fine!

Start writing down all of your extracurricular hours and the different community college classes you have taken (if you've taken any) as you do them, this is going to make the college application process much easier if you have all of them written down with the dates. With the essay writing (if the college your applying to asks for it) you can start as early as the spring of junior year! Definitely make sure to ask for teacher recommendations right when you start your senior year. Time management and planning is very important in this process so keep everything written down and organized!

Join as many clubs as you have time for in your schedule! Joining 2-3 is definitely a good minimum, but definitely join more if you have the opportunity to!

Most schools have students take the PSAT in the fall of their freshman, sophomore, and junior years of high school. They also normally have students do ACT/SAT with the writing parts in the spring of sophomore and junior year, as well as the fall of senior year. In short, your school should facilitate and tell you when to take both tests.

It's important to build a relationship with all of your teachers. Show up to their classes on time, be polite when asking questions, ask questions so they know who you are, and participate in class so they can get familiar with you! When thinking about which teacher to ask for your letter of recommendation, consider the area of study you're most interested in. If you want to go into the STEM field, your math and/or science teacher may be the best choice. Ask your teacher for a letter of recommendation when you're applying for colleges in the fall of your senior year!

Language classes are not super important. Taking more than the required amount can help you become fluent in the language that you're taking (if that's something you're interested in doing) and it can help you look more competitive to colleges. If you don't feel interested in taking more language classes than required, spend your energy elsewhere! Colleges will still love to see that you have an interest in other subjects even if languages aren't your thing!

The important thing to remember though with that being said, is that all of your final grades freshman year will apply to your overall GPA when you graduate from high school.
